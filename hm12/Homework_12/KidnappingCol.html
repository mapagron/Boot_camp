During the period of civil conflict in Colombia that left as many as 220,000 dead, fighting among the military, left-wing guerrillas, and right-wing paramilitaries had left the country on the brink of becoming a failed state by the early 2000s. As both a political measure and a financial resource, the Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia (FARC), the National Liberation Army (ELN), and the Popular Liberation Army (EPL) kidnapped politicians, members of the military, and civilians.
