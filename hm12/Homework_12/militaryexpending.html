2010: Military Expending Increase

The dramatic increase in the military expenditure to end the conflict with the guerrillas also led to an increase of victims of the conflict.

Under the presidency of Andres Pastrana (1998 – 2002), the government initiated and failed a second attempt to negotiate peace, leading to a drastic shift in the government’s approach to the conflict.
A stronger military approach was then adopted while FARC and ELN focused on increasing kidnappings as a method of exerting social and political pressure. This was mainly successful as instead of innocent civilians, the victims were senators, businessmen, and political figures.
